AI receptionist explained

What happens when a customer calls.

A real deployment is not one prompt. It is a controlled call flow connected to the business calendar, lead rules, owner phone, CRM/SMS, and knowledge base.

1. Call routing decisionOption A: AI answers immediately. Option B: phone rings the business first, then forwards to AI after no answer. Option C: after-hours only.
2. Context injectionThe agent receives business name, services, service area, hours, pricing rules, FAQs, emergency rules, calendar rules, and escalation contacts.
3. Conversation + toolsThe agent speaks naturally, handles interruptions, asks intake questions, checks availability, books a slot, sends SMS, or transfers with context.
4. Owner handoffAfter the call: transcript, summary, lead quality, urgency, contact info, and next action go to the owner/CRM.
deployment modes

Three ways to install it, depending on risk and call volume.

This is what clients actually need to see: they can start safe with missed-call rescue, then move to a full receptionist once the script is proven.

☎️ Missed-call only

Business phone rings normally first. If nobody answers after the chosen delay, the call forwards to AI or triggers SMS recovery.

Best for: owners nervous about AI answering everything.

🕒 After-hours front desk

AI answers evenings, weekends, lunch breaks, or staff shortage windows. It books, takes messages, or escalates urgent calls.

Best for: local services losing leads outside working hours.

⚡ Full AI receptionist

AI answers every inbound call, handles common requests, checks calendar, qualifies leads, and transfers only when human judgment is needed.

Best for: high call volume or expensive missed opportunities.

setup timeline

How long it takes to put in place.

For a simple local business, the first working version is measured in hours/days, not months. The long part is not “AI”; it is testing edge cases and making the business rules precise.

Day 0
Collect contextWebsite, services, prices/ranges, business hours, service area, FAQs, booking rules, emergency rules, staff numbers, calendar access.
Day 1
Build first receptionistCreate voice agent, greeting, tone, forbidden claims, intake questions, lead qualification, fallback and transfer rules.
Day 2
Connect toolsPhone number or call forwarding, calendar availability, SMS/email notification, CRM or simple owner alert, transcript storage.
Day 3
Test 20–50 callsNormal call, angry caller, silence, interruption, accent/noise, price question, urgent call, wrong number, booking conflict, transfer failure.
Week 1
Soft launchStart with after-hours or missed-call mode, review transcripts, tune prompt, then expand if the agent handles real calls safely.
